# Travel Expenses and Reimbursement Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the rules, procedures, and expectations related to travel conducted for company business. Its purpose is to ensure consistent, fair, and transparent reimbursement practices while supporting employees in conducting necessary travel efficiently and responsibly.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all full-time, part-time, temporary, and contract employees who travel domestically or internationally for company-related purposes.

## 3. Travel Authorization

* All business travel must be pre-approved by the employee’s direct manager.
* International travel requires approval from both the department manager and the Finance Department.
* Travel bookings must be made through the company’s preferred travel platforms unless exceptions are approved.

## 4. General Guidelines

* Employees must select economical travel options when possible, including economy-class flights, standard hotel rooms, and cost-efficient transportation.
* Loyalty programs may be used, but personal benefits may not influence booking decisions.
* Any deviations from recommended travel guidelines must be documented and justified.

## 5. Reimbursable Expenses

Reimbursable expenses include costs that are reasonable, necessary, and directly related to business travel:

* **Transportation:** airfare, train tickets, mileage reimbursement for personal car use, taxi and rideshare expenses, parking, tolls.
* **Lodging:** standard hotel rooms, taxes, and necessary business amenities.
* **Meals:** daily meal allowances within regional limits; alcohol excluded unless explicitly pre-approved.
* **Business Needs:** mobile data, Wi-Fi, printing, work-related supplies.
* **Other:** visa fees, travel vaccinations, currency exchange fees.

## 6. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

Examples of expenses not covered:

* Personal entertainment, movies, minibar consumption.
* Upgrades (e.g., business class, suite-level rooms) without prior approval.
* Personal travel insurance.
* Fines or penalties (e.g., traffic violations).
* Meals for family members or companions.

## 7. Reimbursement Process

1. Submit an expense report within **30 days** of completing travel.
2. Attach clear, itemized receipts for all expenses.
3. Provide business justification for each item.
4. Manager reviews and approves within 5 business days.
5. Finance validates the submission and processes reimbursement within 10 business days.
