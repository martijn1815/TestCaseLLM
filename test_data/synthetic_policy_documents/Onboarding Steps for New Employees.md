# Onboarding Steps for New Employees

## 1. Purpose

This document outlines a structured onboarding process designed to help new employees integrate into the company, understand their responsibilities, and become productive quickly and confidently.

## 2. Pre-Start Preparation (Before Day 1)

* HR sends the employment contract, welcome information, and first-day agenda.
* Manager prepares role expectations, initial objectives, and information about the team.
* IT prepares the laptop, user accounts, software licenses, and required access permissions.
* A workplace or remote working setup checklist is prepared.

## 3. First Day Agenda

* Welcome meeting with HR to review administrative matters.
* Office tour or virtual orientation session.
* Laptop and system setup with IT support.
* Introduction to team members, assigned mentor/buddy, and key stakeholders.
* Review of company policies, IT security guidelines, and internal communication tools.

## 4. First Week Activities

* Completion of mandatory compliance, safety, and security training.
* Overview sessions with Finance, HR, IT, and other relevant departments.
* Start with small introductory tasks aligned with the role.
* Participation in team meetings and project overviews.
* Schedule 1:1 check-ins with the manager.

## 5. First 30 Days

* Deeper technical or role-specific training.
* Gradual assignment of larger responsibilities.
* Continued mentorship and peer support.
* Review of initial performance and adaptation.

## 6. First 90 Days

* Formal performance check-in with manager.
* Discussion of development goals and training opportunities.
* Integration into long-term projects and workflows.
* Completion of onboarding assessment or feedback survey.

## 7. Ongoing Support

* Access to learning platforms and training resources.
* Regular career development conversations.
* Continued support from HR, IT, and management.
